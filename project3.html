Two channel video, colour and sound.
Cinematography Iain Frengley.
Installation view, Gladstone Court
Edinburgh Art Festival August, 2017

The work Te huka o te tai is a dual channel video work that depictsa split vision of physicality —as located within the environment and then enacted within the creative process. The footage depicts the geothermal active landscape in the mountains surrounding Taipei followed by a solitary figure scribing expressive gestures in charcoal on paper.

This pairing of a landscape teeming with energy and the kinesthetic flow of mark making suggests an innate human tendency to use the environment and objects around us for the task of memory retention and the creation of meaning—whether this be an attempt to retain the histories that a place is founded, or the ability of an artistic response to create a sense stasis within the incessant flow of space and time.
